Łączy Nas Piłka

Redesigning the mobile experience for fans of Polish football.

Role

Research, UX & UI design

Scope

1,5 months

Type

Conceptual

1. Why

DIVING DEEPER

Recent update

“What’s new?
Removed menu button”

I checked in store reviews and recent updates to see how other users perceived the app and how was it being developed.

My intuition was right.

Łączy Nas Piłka mobile app has around since 2015.
The last update of October 5th 2021 bringing deletion of core feature and over 600 reviews averaging 2 stars show how badly it needed a makeover.

Wanting not to miss out on any tournament matches of national Polish football matches, I turned to the biggest football platform in Poland, which also happenes to be their official sponsor.

I figured their app would allow me to be up to date with games, scores and news, but right from the first boot I knew there was something wrong going on with the design.

GOAL

Locate app’s current problem areas.

2. Research

I invited 10 participants to my research.

As opposed to linear development, I opted for a technique known as ‘Hot Potato’. I wanted to have people involved as much as I can, to get a constant stream of feedback.

I conducted qualitative interviews to understand thoughts and feelings towards the app. I also asked them to perform some tasks.

FEEDBACK

“I can’t log in. There’s no side panel.”

“Who even let this out to use. Where’s menu, loging in which is required by the app?”

“Nothing works here.”

FEELINGS

What are your first impressions? How would you describe what you see?

How would you describe experience of finding a team and their information?

TASKS

Can you find a ‘team name’ and all the information about them?

Please describe your thought as you go. What do you think will happen?

Many app store reviews have given me an idea of what can be improved. However, besides adding features I wanted to know what’s most important for someone looking to follow their teams.
I asked them to rank features in order of importance making sense to them.

Creating a user persona allowed me to learn their habits.
I wanted to know when and how they typically access the information, what moods usually accompany them at that time.
Understanding the environment helped better place the app in it.

USER PERSONA

Tomek, 35

Construction worker

Works physically and is often tired. He doesn’t have time, nor the energy to dig through a lot of content.
He has been a football fan since he can remember, and wants to be always up to date on his favourite teams, even during little lunch brakes he has.

Struggles

/ Scroll through a lot of content

/ Visit multiple sites

Wants

/ Quick and easy way to check on his teams

/ Get the latest news

“I’m always in a hurry.”

I wanted to see how the app compares to its competitors.
I chose four most popular. They are big enough to be a direct threat, yet less resourceful to not take the same opportunities.

Doing a direct comparison helped me pin point areas of problem, see what’s missing, how are they all similar and where do they differ.
I collected data from in store reviews.

Creaint SWOT named these aspects directly, so that I won’t work in the dark. It can serve as a reminder of what aspects are worth putting resources in in the future.

GOAL

Bring relevant features and clear, intuitive design.

3. Ideation

One of the requirements was that everything is easily and quickly accessible. I wanted to design the user flow so that one needs to do the least amount of clicking and backing to main page to access different features. I achieved that by grouping functionalities by categories.

To not get fixated on a particular design I used ‘Crazy 8’s’ method. It allowed me to come up with many desings and not critique them too much at this point.

However, testing showed that my idea turned out to be wrong. It didn’t solve the issue, just presented whole new ones. So I dove back into wireframing.

I showed the following designs to users one by one.
I wanted to know what they liked, what they didn’t like from all of these designs. It turned out that all of the useres settled on the same deisgn.

A/B TESTING

Here I tested different layouts and access.

WHY

They looked familiar. In favor of habbits developed from using other apps.

Big graphics. Eye catching and engaging photos.

Easy access to data.

Their only reservations were that the labels weren’t as clear, and they’d like to see how many articles are there, and their scroll indication.

CONCLUSIONS

Don’t be scared to start over.

Even the best ideas may not pan out the way you wanted. There is no point in trying to make something work, that’s wrong in its core.

Don’t overthink.

Let your creativity flow. Allow yourself for imperfections. Sometimes the best solutions come when you least expect them.

Get inspired.

Studying others’ designs can make yours more familiar. The less fatigue user feels, the happier he is.

Test, test, test.

Test everything always. There’s no such thing as too much testing.
